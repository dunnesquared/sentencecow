Multiple sentences in one
Occasionally, the program will combine two complete sentences when it shouldn't. This can happen when there is no space right after a terminating punctuation mark, as with footnote references. e.g. The full moon is an ecclesiastical full moon determined by reference to a conventional cycle.[1] (Wikipedia: "Computus")
The fix for this is tricky, so the easiest thing to do would be to remove the 'offending' characters from the text and see whether that fixes things.

Divided sentence
Sometimes detected sentences are shorter than they actually are, especially when quotes and abbreviations are involved. To combine to two consecutive too-short sentences, click 'Merge next' on the sentence you want to be joined with the one following it.
